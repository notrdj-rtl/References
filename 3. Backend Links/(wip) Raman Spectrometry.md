##### Concepts:
**What did A.smekal find in 1923?** 
	inelastic light scattering
**Explain inelastic light scattering in more detail**
	the idea of photon colliding with material and exchange energy that produce different wavelengths than original light
**What did Raman and Krishnan find in 1928 (Nobel in 1930)**
	Secondary radiation from neat solvents (photons bounce)
**3 Ways lasers can encounter material**
	absorbed, transmitted, scattered
**Example of Elastic vs ineleastic scattering**
	elastic = rayleigh (green)
	inelastic = raman (red/blue)
**what is virtual state**
	short lived unobservable state
**What happens when electrons move from virtual to regular state**
	light emitted
**What happens when electrons move from virtual to excited state**
	raman shift, aka the fingerprint of a molecule. 
**Explain raman shift in more detail**
	Depends on natural frequency of vibrations. Frequency depends on mass of molecule. 
	Example: Heavy molecule = long wavelength, low frequency and energy
**The target of Raman Spectroscopy**
	material specific pattern of the spectrum
	aka, material identification under conditions
**Wavenumber as it relates to Raman**
	light's frequency (or energy) using the reciprocal of wavelength.
	differences in wavenumber correspond directly to differences in molecular vibrational energy. 
	This makes Raman spectra easier to interpret than using wavelengths, regardless of laser inherit wavelength.
